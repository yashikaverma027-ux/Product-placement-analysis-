Strategic Product Visibility and Placement Analysis Using Interactive Data Science
1.	Introduction

 Modern retail success depends heavily on data-driven product visibility to maximize revenue and enhance the shopper journey. The specific placement of an item—whether in a standard aisle, the store entrance, or promotional end-caps—serves as a primary driver of consumer purchasing decisions.

 This project investigates the multifaceted relationship between merchandising tactics, shopper demographics, and competitive pricing. By leveraging Tableau for advanced visual analytics and Flask for web-based deployment, this study provides actionable insights into optimizing store layouts for increased profitability.


2.	Problem Statement 

Retailers frequently struggle to balance an intuitive store flow with a layout that maximizes high-margin sales. The core challenge addressed is:

 How do store positioning, pricing fluctuations, and specific customer segments dictate sales outcomes? By resolving this, businesses can:
 ● Refine localized placement strategies.
 ● Enhance the visibility of underperforming categories. 
● Calibrate pricing against market competitors. 
● Boost total store conversion rates.



3.	Project Objectives 

The primary goals of this analysis include:

 1. Quantifying the link between shelf location and sales volume.
 2. Identifying how demographic groups vary in their buying patterns. 
3. Benchmarking internal pricing against competitor data. 
4. Measuring the lift generated by promotional status. 
5. Constructing an interactive Flask-integrated dashboard for stakeholders.


4 .Data Acquisition
 The dataset used in this project was obtained from Kaggle, a well-known platform for public datasets.

 Dataset Source: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/amitvkulkarni/impact of-product-positioning-on-sales
 The dataset contains retail sales information including: 
• Product category
 • Product position in store
 • Sales volume
 • Price
 • Competitor price
 • Consumer demographics 
• Promotion status 
• Seasonal demand
 • Foot traffic
 This dataset helps analyse how different retail factors influence product sales.


5	.Data Variable Description

Column Name	Functional Description
Product Category	Classification (e.g., Electronics, Clothing, Food).
Product Position	Specific location: Aisle, End-cap, or Front of Store.
Sales Volume	Total units sold during the period.
Consumer Demographics	Target groups: Students, Families, Seniors, etc..
Foot Traffic	Level of customer movement (Low, Medium, High).




6.Data Preparation Pipeline

 To ensure analytical integrity, the following cleaning steps were implemented: 

● Null Handling: Incomplete records were scrubbed or imputed to maintain dataset balance.

 ● Type Casting: Metrics like Price and Volume were standardized as numerical floats/integers.

 ● Structural Validation: The data was audited for duplicates and categorical consistency.

8.Tableau Story & Strategic Insights

The analysis revealed several critical "Story Points" regarding retail behavior: 
● Point 1: Category Dominance
•	Clothing emerged as the top performer with an average sales volume of 1830.1.

•	 Electronics followed closely (1748.6), while Food showed the lowest average volume (1727.7).


 ● Point 2: The Traffic/Position Synergy 

•	Items in High Traffic areas, specifically at the Front of Store, achieved peak sales (approx. 1811.7).

•	 Interestingly, Aisle placement performed robustly even in low traffic scenarios (1900.5), suggesting high "destination" demand for certain items. 

● Point 3: Treemap Analysis of High-Performance Zones 

•	 The highest specific conversion was Clothing located at the Front of Store (1923.7). 

•	 This confirms that "Prime Real Estate" in-store significantly boosts high-interest categories.
